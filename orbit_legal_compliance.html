Section 01
Legal overview
Critical requirement
Legal and compliance infrastructure must be in place before any creator connects their social media account. Once you touch real OAuth tokens and personal data, you are legally obligated under GDPR, CCPA, and each platform's developer terms. None of this can be retroactively applied.

Legal entity requirements

Immediate
Incorporate as LLC or C-Corp
Separate personal liability from business liability. C-Corp preferred if you plan to raise investment. Delaware C-Corp is the US standard for VC-backed companies.
Immediate
EIN & business bank account
Separate business finances from personal from day one. Required for Stripe integration, contractor payments, and any investor due diligence.
Before launch
IP assignment agreements
All engineers and contractors must sign IP assignment agreements confirming all work product belongs to Orbit. Critical before any fundraising conversation.

Document priority matrix

Must have before first user

Privacy Policy — legally required under GDPR, CCPA, and all platform developer agreements
Terms of Service — governs the relationship between Orbit and every user
Data Processing Agreement (DPA) — required for EU users under GDPR Article 28
Cookie Policy — required in EU/UK for any analytics or tracking
OAuth consent screens — each platform requires specific consent language

Must have within 30 days of launch

Subscription agreement — governs paid tier billing, cancellation, and refunds
Agency agreement template — covers the Mission Control multi-creator access relationship
Acceptable use policy — defines prohibited uses of Orbit and creator data
DMCA policy — required if your platform hosts any creator content thumbnails
Bug bounty / vulnerability disclosure policy — security researcher safe harbour
Section 02
Pre-launch compliance checklist
Register legal entity (LLC or C-Corp)
Delaware C-Corp recommended if raising capital. Use Stripe Atlas, Clerky, or a startup attorney. Cost: ~$500–$1,500.
Before any development
Draft and publish Privacy Policy
Must cover: data collected, how it's used, third-party sharing, retention periods, user rights (access, deletion, portability), and contact information. Must be accessible at /privacy before any OAuth flow.
Before first OAuth connection
Draft and publish Terms of Service
Covers user obligations, IP ownership, prohibited uses, limitation of liability, dispute resolution, and governing law. Users must actively accept (checkbox, not just browsing).
Before account creation
Apply for platform developer access
Meta Graph API (Instagram), TikTok for Developers — both require app review and approval before production access. Instagram requires Business Verification. TikTok requires a detailed use case review. Timeline: 2–6 weeks each. Start immediately.
Start now — long lead time
Implement OAuth consent screens with correct language
Each platform requires specific consent language in the OAuth flow explaining exactly what data Orbit will access and why. Must match exactly what your Privacy Policy states. Mismatches = platform ban.
Before beta
Implement explicit benchmarking consent
Creators must actively opt in to the anonymized benchmarking pool — this cannot be a default. Requires a separate, clearly worded consent checkbox with a plain-English explanation of what their data is used for in aggregate.
Before Constellation feature
Encrypt all OAuth tokens at rest
AES-256 encryption for all stored access and refresh tokens. Tokens must never appear in logs, error messages, or API responses. Use AWS Secrets Manager or equivalent for key management.
Before any OAuth storage
Build data deletion pipeline
Users must be able to delete their account and all associated data. GDPR requires deletion within 30 days of request. Build the pipeline before launch — retrofitting deletion is painful. Facebook also requires a Data Deletion Callback URL for any Instagram integration.
Required by Meta for Instagram API
Set up Stripe and publish billing terms
Subscription terms must state: pricing, billing cycle, what happens at cancellation, refund policy, and how price changes are communicated. Stripe requires these be visible before checkout.
Before paid tier
GDPR consent management (EU users)
Cookie consent banner, consent records for data processing, clear mechanism for users to exercise rights (access, portability, erasure, restriction). Even if targeting US creators initially, UK/EU creators will sign up and you must be compliant.
Before any EU user
IP assignment agreements for all engineers
Every person who writes code must sign an IP assignment agreement before their first commit. This is non-negotiable for any future fundraising, acquisition, or partnership.
Before first line of code
Cyber liability insurance
Given the sensitivity of OAuth tokens and creator data, cyber liability insurance is strongly recommended. Cost: ~$1,200–$2,500/year for a startup. Covers breach response, legal costs, and notification requirements.
Within 60 days of launch
SOC 2 Type II — begin preparation
Not required at launch but will be asked for by enterprise agencies and brand partners. Begin implementing the control framework from day one — it's far easier than retrofitting. Target certification within 18 months.
Month 6–18
Section 03
Privacy policy — key clauses

This section outlines the required clauses for Orbit's Privacy Policy. Each clause must be written in plain English, not legalese. GDPR requires policies be "concise, transparent, intelligible and easily accessible."

Required sections

Data we collect

Account data: Name, email, profile photo, timezone.

Connected platform data: When you connect Instagram or TikTok, we access: your public profile information, follower count, post metadata (captions, hashtags, timestamps), post performance metrics (reach, impressions, likes, comments, saves, shares). We do not access your private messages, payment information, or the content of direct messages.

Usage data: How you interact with Orbit — features used, session duration, click patterns. Used to improve the product.

Benchmarking data (opt-in only): If you explicitly consent, anonymized and aggregated performance metrics may be included in our benchmarking pool. Your individual data is never exposed to other users.

How we use your data

We use your data to:
— Provide the Orbit analytics service
— Calculate your Gravity Score
— Generate AI-powered coaching insights
— Detect Escape Velocity events and send alerts
— Generate trend intelligence relevant to your niche
— Produce benchmarking reports (with your consent)
— Send product notifications and weekly briefings

We do not sell your data. We do not use your data for advertising. We do not share your individual data with third parties except as required to operate the service (Anthropic AI, AWS infrastructure).

Data retention

Active accounts: Data retained for the duration of your account plus 30 days after deletion request.

Performance metrics: Up to 24 months of historical data. Older data is aggregated and anonymized.

Benchmarking pool data: Anonymized aggregate data may be retained indefinitely. Your individual contribution is removed within 30 days of opt-out or account deletion.

Backups: Backup snapshots retained for 7 days. Deleted account data is purged from backups within 30 days.

Your rights (GDPR / CCPA)

You have the right to:
— Access all data we hold about you (export within 30 days)
— Correct inaccurate data
— Delete your account and all associated data
— Port your data in machine-readable format
— Restrict how we process your data
— Withdraw consent for benchmarking at any time
— Object to processing for direct marketing

To exercise any right: privacy@orbitapp.io
Response time: within 30 days.

Third-party data processors

Disclosure requirement
Every third-party service that touches creator data must be disclosed in your Privacy Policy and covered by a Data Processing Agreement. This includes Anthropic (AI insights), AWS (infrastructure), Stripe (billing), Auth0/Supabase (authentication), and any analytics tools.

Section 04
Terms of service — key clauses

Acceptable use

You may not use Orbit to:
— Scrape, harvest, or export data for use outside the platform
— Share your account credentials with others
— Attempt to reverse-engineer or extract the benchmarking algorithm
— Use Orbit data to build competing products
— Misrepresent your Gravity Score or benchmark rank to third parties in a materially misleading way
— Circumvent the platform's rate limits or access controls

Intellectual property

Your data: You own your content and performance data. You grant Orbit a limited licence to process it to provide the service.

Benchmarking pool: By opting in, you grant Orbit a licence to use your anonymized, aggregated data indefinitely for benchmarking. You can withdraw this consent at any time.

Orbit IP: The Gravity Score algorithm, Orbit Path model, Escape Velocity detection system, and all product features are proprietary to Orbit. You may not reproduce or commercialise them.

AI-generated content

Orbit's AI insights are for informational purposes only. They do not constitute professional business, financial, or legal advice. Results are based on historical data and are not guarantees of future performance. Orbit is not liable for decisions made based on AI coaching outputs.

Limitation of liability

Orbit's maximum liability to any user is limited to the fees paid in the 12 months preceding the claim. Orbit is not liable for: indirect or consequential losses, loss of revenue or profits, data loss from third-party platform API outages, or decisions made based on Orbit's analytics or AI outputs.

Orbit does not guarantee platform uptime or data freshness. Platform API outages (Instagram, TikTok, etc.) are outside Orbit's control.

Subscription & billing

Subscriptions auto-renew monthly. You may cancel at any time — cancellation takes effect at the end of the current billing period. No refunds for partial months.

Price changes will be communicated 30 days in advance by email. Continued use after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the new price.

Downgrading to a lower tier is effective immediately. Upgrading is effective immediately and prorated.

Termination

Orbit may terminate or suspend accounts that violate these terms, engage in fraudulent activity, or abuse the platform. We will provide 7 days notice except in cases of fraud or security breach.

Upon termination, you may request a data export within 30 days. After 30 days, all data will be deleted in accordance with the Privacy Policy.

Data minimisation rules

What to collect
Only collect what the product actively uses. If a metric isn't displayed or used in a calculation, don't store it. Every data point stored is a liability — both legally and in terms of storage costs and security surface area.
Never collect or store
Private messages from any platform · Password credentials in plain text · Payment card details (Stripe handles this) · Government IDs · Precise location data · Data on minors under 18

Data subject request process

Every data subject request (access, deletion, portability, correction) must be fulfilled within 30 days. Build these workflows before launch.

Access request
Data export
Generate a JSON/CSV export of all data held: account info, performance metrics, AI insights, consent records. Available in-app via Settings → Privacy → Export my data.
Deletion request
Right to erasure
Soft delete immediately (account inactive), hard delete within 30 days. Remove from benchmarking pool. Purge from backups within 30 days. Meta requires a Data Deletion Callback URL — must be implemented.
Portability request
Data portability
Export in machine-readable format (JSON). Includes all performance data, Gravity Score history, AI insights, and consent records. Must not include third-party platform data (that belongs to the platform).
Section 06
OAuth & platform compliance

Each social platform has its own developer terms that must be followed independently of GDPR/CCPA. Violating platform terms results in immediate API access revocation — a catastrophic business risk.

Platform-specific requirements

PlatformKey requirementProhibited usesReview processRisk level
Instagram (Meta) Business verification required. Data Deletion Callback URL mandatory. 60-day data retention limit on some metrics. Cannot store user content (images/videos) beyond 90 days. Cannot use data to build competing products. Cannot sell or transfer data. App review 2–4 weeks. Must demonstrate legitimate use case with working demo. High — Meta reserves right to revoke access at any time
TikTok Most restrictive API. Limited analytics data without Partner Program approval. Must apply for Research API separately. Cannot store TikTok video content. Cannot use data for ad targeting. Cannot share data with third parties. Developer review 4–6 weeks. Partner Program review is additional and may take months. Very high — API access is fragile and frequently restricted
YouTube (Google) Must comply with Google API Services User Data Policy. Limited quota (10,000 units/day by default — request increase). Cannot store YouTube user data beyond what's needed for the service. Must delete data when user revokes access. Google OAuth verification required for sensitive scopes. 4–6 weeks. Medium — well-documented, more stable than TikTok
TikTok API risk
TikTok's API is the most restrictive and unstable of all platforms. They have a history of suddenly restricting access, changing terms, and limiting data availability. Build Orbit's architecture so it degrades gracefully if TikTok API access is lost — the platform should still function fully for Instagram users. Never let a single platform API failure break the product.

OAuth implementation requirements

Request minimum scopes only — only request the specific OAuth permissions you actively use. Requesting more than needed is a Terms violation and a red flag in App Review.
Token rotation — implement automatic refresh token rotation. Never let tokens expire silently — alert creators when re-authentication is needed.
Revocation handling — when a user revokes access at the platform level, handle the webhook gracefully. Mark the connection as revoked, stop data collection, do not attempt to re-authenticate without user action.
Webhook verification — verify all incoming platform webhooks using the platform-provided signature. Reject unsigned or incorrectly signed requests.
Rate limit compliance — implement exponential backoff and respect platform rate limits strictly. Exceeding limits results in temporary bans; repeated violations result in permanent revocation.
Data deletion callback — Meta requires a publicly accessible endpoint that processes data deletion requests when a user deletes their Facebook/Instagram account. This is mandatory and tested during App Review.
Section 07
GDPR & CCPA compliance

GDPR (EU/UK)

Applies to any EU or UK resident who uses Orbit, regardless of where Orbit is incorporated. Fines up to €20M or 4% of annual global turnover.

Legal basis for processing

Contract performance — processing required to provide the analytics service (primary basis)
Legitimate interests — product improvement, security monitoring, fraud prevention
Consent — benchmarking pool participation, marketing emails, non-essential cookies

Required implementations

Cookie consent banner with granular controls (necessary, functional, analytics)
Consent records stored with timestamp and version of policy accepted
Data Processing Agreements with all processors (see Section 03)
EU data residency option for EU users (AWS eu-west-1)
Designated Data Protection contact (can be founder initially)
72-hour breach notification to supervisory authority

CCPA (California)

Applies to California residents. Applies if Orbit has >$25M revenue, >50K California users, or derives >50% of revenue from selling California consumer data. Even below thresholds, good practice to comply.

Required disclosures

Categories of personal information collected
Purposes for which personal information is used
Categories of third parties data is shared with
"Do Not Sell My Personal Information" link (even if you don't sell)

User rights under CCPA

Right to know what data is collected
Right to delete personal information
Right to opt out of sale (Orbit does not sell data)
Right to non-discrimination for exercising rights

Practical steps

Add "Do Not Sell" link to footer even if not legally required — signals good faith
Ensure Privacy Policy covers all CCPA disclosure categories
CCPA and GDPR rights overlap significantly — one data subject request process covers both
Section 08
Security standards

Minimum security requirements (pre-launch)

TLS 1.3 on all endpoints
No unencrypted HTTP anywhere. Redirect all HTTP to HTTPS.
AES-256 encryption at rest
Database encryption, S3 server-side encryption, encrypted backups.
VPC with private subnets
All databases in private subnets. No direct internet access to RDS.
MFA for all admin access
AWS console, database access, and Orbit admin panel all require MFA.
Secrets management
All API keys, OAuth secrets, and credentials in AWS Secrets Manager. Never in code or environment variables in plain text.

Incident response plan

If a breach occurs
0–1 hour: Contain the breach. Revoke compromised credentials. Take affected systems offline if necessary.
1–4 hours: Assess scope. What data was accessed? How many users affected?
4–24 hours: Notify legal counsel. Begin GDPR 72-hour notification clock if EU users affected.
24–72 hours: Notify supervisory authority (GDPR). Prepare user notification.
72hr+: Notify affected users. Post-incident review. Remediation.
