01
The opportunity
The creator economy is a $500 billion market. The tools built to serve it were designed for someone else.

Two hundred million people worldwide now consider content creation their primary career. Collectively, they generate more cultural influence than any media network in history — and they are doing it largely blind. They know their follower count. They know their like counts. What almost none of them know is what any of it actually means, how they compare to their peers, or what to do next to grow.

Every analytics tool in the market was built for brands managing social channels, not creators building audiences. Later, Sprout Social, Metricool, Iconosquare — all of them treat analytics as a reporting function. They describe what happened. None of them tell you what to do about it.

The result is a gap that nobody has filled: a creator-native intelligence platform that combines cross-platform analytics, true niche benchmarking, and AI coaching grounded in a creator's own performance data. That is precisely what Orbit is.

02
The product
Orbit is the first platform to tell creators not just what happened — but what it means, how they rank, and exactly what to do next.

The core product is built around five features that collectively define a new category. Each is named using the orbital mechanics metaphor that runs through the entire brand, and each solves a specific problem that no existing tool addresses.

Gravity Score™ is a proprietary composite metric — the first single number that captures a creator's true audience pull rather than vanity metrics. It combines return viewer rate, save and share depth, comment quality, cross-platform following overlap, and follower velocity into a 0–100 score. A creator with 200K followers and a Gravity Score of 84 is provably more valuable to a brand than one with 800K and a score of 31. Gravity Score becomes the number creators reference in brand pitch negotiations and the standard the industry starts asking about.

Escape Velocity is Orbit's real-time breakout detection system. When a piece of content crosses 2.5× a creator's baseline engagement rate, Orbit flags it instantly, reverse-engineers why it happened — format, timing, audio, audience segment — and delivers a specific 48-hour replication playbook. The 48-hour window after a breakout post is the most valuable creative moment a creator has, and nobody else is helping them capitalise on it.

Constellation View is niche-specific peer benchmarking. Not comparison against all creators — comparison against creators who match on niche, following tier, platform mix, and posting frequency. A fitness creator finally knows whether their 6.8% engagement rate is exceptional or average for someone at their tier. That context transforms raw data into strategic clarity.

Dark Matter is trend intelligence filtered to the creator's niche. Trending audio, formats, and hashtags surfaced 2–3 weeks before they reach mainstream trending pages — when there is still time to be early. Delivered weekly as a briefing and in real time when a signal crosses a momentum threshold.

Mission Control is the agency layer — a roster management dashboard giving talent managers a single screen view across every creator they manage, with escape velocity alerts, trajectory changes, and auto-generated brand deal performance reports. This is the feature that makes Orbit a B2B product as well as a creator tool, and it is the foundation of our highest-value revenue tier.

Why we win
Most analytics startups spend their first two years trying to acquire the data and relationships we already have.

Orbit is being built from within an existing talent agency that manages 20–50 lifestyle and fitness creators. This is not a small advantage. It is a structural moat that cannot be quickly replicated by any competitor, funded or otherwise, because it took years of relationship-building to create.

The benchmarking cold-start problem — the hardest challenge for any analytics platform — does not exist for us. We launch with real, first-party performance data from a real creator roster. Constellation benchmarking is meaningful on day one. Our AI models are trained on real niche data, not synthetic approximations. The Gravity Score is calibrated against actual fitness and lifestyle creators, not generic proxies.

The trust problem — the second hardest challenge for any tool asking creators to connect their social accounts — also does not exist for us. These creators already trust their talent managers. That trust transfers directly to Orbit. Our beta is not a cold acquisition funnel. It is a warm conversation with creators who already know us.

Beyond the founding advantage, Orbit's architecture creates a compounding data network effect. Every creator who opts into the benchmarking pool makes the Constellation rankings more precise. More precise rankings drive better AI recommendations. Better recommendations drive growth. Faster-growing creators refer other creators. Each turn of the flywheel widens the intelligence gap between Orbit and any competitor attempting to enter the space.

There is also a timing advantage that cannot be overstated. No AI-native creator analytics product exists at this price point for this vertical. The window to establish Gravity Score as the industry standard metric — before a well-funded competitor recognises the same opportunity — is measured in months, not years. We are already building.

05
Business model
Three tiers. Two motions. One flywheel. The business model is designed to grow in two directions simultaneously — bottom-up from individual creators and top-down through agency partnerships.

The Creator tier at $29/month is the freemium conversion product. Free entry gives creators the Gravity Score and basic insights — enough to demonstrate value and create the activation moment. Paid unlocks the full benchmarking suite, Escape Velocity alerts, and Dark Matter briefings. The conversion mechanic is a feature gate at the moment of maximum curiosity: a creator sees their niche rank but has to upgrade to see the gap analysis. At $29, the conversion is a low-friction decision.

The Pro tier at $79/month is the target ARPU for our core creator segment. It unlocks Orbit Path trajectory modeling, content briefs from Dark Matter signals, and the shareable Gravity Score card for brand pitches. The Pro tier has the best unit economics in the product — at roughly 86% contribution margin after AI API costs, it generates significant cash per account. A creator who upgrades to Pro is signalling that they are serious about growth, which also means they are a high-retention user.

The Agency tier at $499/month is the profit engine. Each agency account covers the AI and infrastructure costs of approximately 30 Creator tier accounts. At 86% contribution margin, two or three agency clients essentially fund the entire variable cost base of the platform. The agency tier also creates a structural acquisition channel — each agency onboarded brings 10–50 creators with it, at zero acquisition cost per creator.

On our base case financial model — bootstrapped, 2 engineers, conservative growth assumptions — Orbit reaches break-even at month 14 and exits year two at approximately $200K ARR from Orbit revenue alone, supplemented by the ongoing agency business. With the capital we are seeking, break-even accelerates to month 9 and year-two ARR exceeds $400K.

Risks we are honest about
Every investor memo should name the risks plainly. Here are ours, and why we believe they are manageable.
High
Platform API dependency. Meta and TikTok can restrict or revoke API access with limited notice. This is the most consequential risk in the business. Our mitigation: build the architecture so Orbit degrades gracefully if any single platform API is lost — the product still works with remaining platforms. We maintain strict compliance with platform terms and never request more scopes than we use. We will have multiple platforms live before public launch so no single API failure is catastrophic.
High
A well-funded competitor enters the space. If a Later or Metricool adds AI coaching, or a new entrant raises $5M specifically for creator analytics, our differentiation narrows. Our mitigation: the data moat is the primary defence. A new entrant needs 12–18 months to build a comparable benchmarking dataset from scratch. We will be 18 months ahead on the data before they start. We also have the Gravity Score trademark and the agency acquisition flywheel — structural advantages beyond features.
Medium
Creator churn if early AI insights are too generic. The product's retention depends entirely on the AI coaching feeling genuinely intelligent and specific. If the first insights a creator sees feel like generic tips they could have googled, they will not return. Our mitigation: we have an explicit quality standard — every AI output must reference specific data points from that creator's own performance history. Generic outputs are a product failure, not a product feature. We will instrument feedback from day one and treat low ratings as critical bugs.
Medium
Sales cycle length for agency tier. Agency partnerships are our highest-value revenue line but require a different sales motion than creator self-serve. Agencies are slower to evaluate and onboard. Our mitigation: our own agency is the proof of concept and the case study. We are not cold-calling agencies — we are showing them the product we use ourselves, with data from creators we manage. The first few external agency wins will be warm referrals, not cold outbound.

The feature matrix across every competitor in the space confirms what we see every day in practice: there is no AI-native, creator-native, niche-specific intelligence platform at this price point. The window to build it and establish the Gravity Score as the industry standard metric is open. It will not stay open indefinitely.
