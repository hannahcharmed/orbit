Section 01
Competitive landscape

The creator analytics space is crowded with tools but almost entirely populated by products built for brands and agencies — not creators. This is Orbit's fundamental opportunity. Every major competitor shares the same flaw: they treat analytics as a reporting function rather than a coaching function.

Category 1
Social media management tools
Later, Hootsuite, Buffer, Sprout Social. Built for scheduling and publishing. Analytics is a secondary feature. Designed for marketing teams managing brand accounts — not individual creators optimising their own growth.
Category 2
Influencer intelligence platforms
HypeAuditor, Modash, Traackr, CreatorIQ. Built for brands discovering and vetting creators. Data is viewed from the outside — what does this creator look like to a brand? Not useful for the creator themselves.
Category 3
Native platform analytics
Instagram Insights, TikTok Analytics, YouTube Studio. Free and always on, but siloed to one platform, no cross-platform view, no benchmarking, no AI coaching. The baseline every creator already has.
The whitespace Orbit owns
There is currently no product that combines (1) creator-native analytics, (2) true niche benchmarking, (3) AI coaching grounded in the creator's own data, and (4) a talent agency management layer — in a single platform built specifically for lifestyle and fitness creators. That gap is Orbit's entire reason for existing.

Section 07
Competitive strategy

How to win

Strategy 1 — Own the metric
Make the Gravity Score™ the metric creators reference when talking about their value. When a creator tells a brand "my Gravity Score is 78" in a pitch, that's Orbit in every conversation. File the trademark. Build the brand story around it. Get it into creator vocabulary before anyone can copy it.
Strategy 2 — Niche before broad
Resist the temptation to expand to all niches early. Being the best tool for fitness and lifestyle creators beats being average for everyone. When fitness creators recommend tools to each other, Orbit's name will be the one they say — because it was built specifically for them.
Strategy 3 — Speed of insight
Competitors respond to data — Orbit responds to creators. The moat is not the features, it's the compounding data advantage. Every creator who opts into benchmarking makes the platform smarter. Build fast, iterate with the agency roster, and widen the intelligence gap before a funded competitor can catch up.
Strategy 4 — The agency flywheel
No competitor has an agency-creator dual product. Use this to create an acquisition flywheel: agencies bring creators onto Orbit, creators refer other creators, those creators' managers want Mission Control. One agency partnership = 10–50 warm creator signups with zero paid acquisition cost.
