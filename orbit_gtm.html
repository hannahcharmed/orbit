Section 01
GTM strategy overview

Orbit's go-to-market strategy is built around a single structural advantage most competitors can't replicate: we already have the creators, the data, and the trust. The GTM plan is not about acquiring users from scratch — it's about converting an existing agency roster into an evangelized user base, then using that momentum to expand outward.

Core GTM thesis
"Don't build an audience and then prove the product works. Prove the product works with our existing audience, then let them build the next audience for us."

Strategic pillars

Pillar 1
Agency-led distribution
The agency roster is the launch pad. 50+ creators who already trust us become the first users, case study subjects, and word-of-mouth engine. Zero cold acquisition required to reach meaningful scale.
Pillar 2
Creator-led virality
Creators talk to creators. When a fitness creator shares their Gravity Score in a brand pitch or posts about their Orbit Path trajectory on Stories, that's free acquisition. Every Orbit touchpoint is a potential share moment.
Pillar 3
Niche authority first
Own lifestyle and fitness completely before expanding. Being the definitive tool for one vertical is more defensible than being average for all verticals. Niche authority creates word-of-mouth density.

GTM motion type

Product-led growth (PLG)
Freemium entry point drives self-serve adoption. The product itself demonstrates value — creators see their Gravity Score, get their first AI insight, and convert without a sales call. Viral mechanics built into the product (shareable scorecards, public rankings).
Agency-led sales (ALS)
The Agency tier ($499/mo) is a direct sales motion. Target: talent agencies managing 10+ creators. High-touch onboarding, white-glove setup of roster data. One agency sale = instant 10–50 creator accounts on the platform.

Target segments

Agency-managed fitness creators
Who: Fitness and lifestyle creators with 50K–2M followers managed by talent agencies

Pain: Posting consistently but not understanding what's working

Secondary segments — Year 2+

Segment C
Talent agencies
Independent talent management companies managing 10–100 creators. High-value Agency tier customers. Direct outreach + conference presence. One conversion = large MRR impact.
Segment D
Wellness & lifestyle creators
Adjacent niche expansion. Mindfulness, mental health, self-care creators who share the lifestyle audience. Natural extension once fitness niche is saturated.
Segment E
Brand partnerships teams
Future segment: brand-side teams who want to use Gravity Score data in creator selection. Opens B2B data licensing revenue stream without building a full marketplace.

Acquisition channels

Owned channels

Orbit's own social presence Low cost
Instagram and TikTok accounts showing real creator analytics, Gravity Score callouts, and Dark Matter signals. Orbit itself becomes a creator — modeling the behavior it teaches. Target: 10K followers by month 6.
Metric: followers, profile link clicks → free signups
SEO & content marketing Medium effort
Long-form content targeting "how to grow on Instagram as a fitness creator", "best posting times for fitness content", "how to increase engagement rate". Each article links to a free Gravity Score tool.
Metric: organic search traffic → free trial signups
Email marketing Low cost
Weekly Dark Matter briefing available as a free email newsletter — subscribers get top 3 trending signals for fitness niche. Builds owned audience and converts to full platform. Target: 5,000 subscribers by month 9.
Metric: email subscribers → free signups → paid conversion

Paid & partnership channels

Creator seeding Medium cost
Provide free Pro access to 20–30 mid-tier fitness creators (100K–500K) in exchange for authentic organic content. These creators talk to creators — their endorsement carries 10× the weight of paid ads in this community.
Metric: referred signups, coupon code attribution
Podcast sponsorships Medium cost
Sponsor 3–5 creator-focused podcasts: Creator Science, The Influencer Podcast, Earn Your Happy. Host-read ads with a free Gravity Score offer. These audiences are Orbit's exact ICP.
Metric: promo code redemptions → signups
Paid social (Meta) Higher cost
Meta ads targeting: fitness creator interest segments, Instagram/TikTok creator tools audience, lookalikes from converted users. Lead with "Get your free Gravity Score" — not "try our platform". Begin month 4 once case studies are ready.
Metric: CPA target <$35 for free signup, <$120 for paid conversion

Section 09
Community strategy

The most defensible long-term moat in the creator tool space is not features — it's community. A creator community built around Orbit becomes a data network, a referral engine, and a product feedback loop simultaneously.

Orbit community — Phase 1

Private Discord / Slack
The Constellation
Invite-only community for Pro and Agency users. Channels: #dark-matter-signals (weekly trend drops), #escape-velocity-wins (celebrate breakout posts), #gravity-score-flex (share your score). Orbit team is active — not just a forum.
Creator advisory board
5 founding creators
5 high-profile fitness/lifestyle creators who co-develop the product with us. Given early access to all features, equity-adjacent compensation (not cash), and public recognition. Their input shapes the roadmap and their posts drive acquisition.

Creator program — Phase 2

Orbit Certified
Creator intelligence certification
A free certification program for creators who complete Orbit's education modules. Certificate is shareable on social and in brand pitches. Creates a credentialed community and drives top-of-funnel through certificate sharing.
Agency network
Orbit Agency Council
Quarterly roundtables for Agency tier users. Share anonymized industry benchmarks, preview upcoming features, shape the agency product roadmap. Creates high-value retention for the $499/mo tier.
