Section 01
Onboarding principles
The single most important moment
The onboarding flow is the product's most important conversion moment. A creator who sees their Gravity Score for the first time and thinks "I didn't know that" is a retained user. A creator who connects their account and sees a blank dashboard is gone. Every design decision in onboarding must serve one goal: time to first insight under 3 minutes.

Design principles

Principle 1
Value before friction
Show the creator something genuinely useful before asking for anything beyond basic signup. The Gravity Score appears the moment the first platform connects — no waiting screen, no "we're crunching your data" delay over 60 seconds.
Principle 2
One ask at a time
Never present multiple decisions simultaneously. Each screen has exactly one primary action. Progress indicators show creators where they are — never more than 5 steps to the first insight.
Principle 3
Every empty state teaches
Empty states are not failures — they're opportunities. Every metric that can't yet be calculated gets a plain-English explanation of what it is and what will populate it. No blank boxes.
Principle 4
The wow moment is non-negotiable
Every creator must experience at least one "I didn't know that" moment during onboarding. For most creators, this is seeing their Gravity Score for the first time and their niche rank. Design explicitly for this reaction.
Principle 5
Consent is respected, not buried
The benchmarking opt-in is presented clearly and honestly — not buried in settings or pre-checked. Creators who opt in knowingly are more trusting users. Creators who feel tricked churn and leave bad reviews.
Principle 6
Mobile-first, always
Creators will onboard on their phones. Every screen is designed for mobile first — large touch targets, thumb-friendly layouts, no horizontal scrolling. Desktop is a secondary consideration.
Section 02
Step-by-step onboarding flow
1
Landing / acquisition
The hook — "Get your free Gravity Score"
0 seconds — before signup
The primary CTA on the landing page is not "Sign up" or "Try Orbit" — it's "Get your free Gravity Score." This is specific, personal, and outcome-oriented. A creator doesn't know what Orbit is but they immediately want to know what their Gravity Score is.
Landing page copy
"Every creator has a gravitational pull. Yours is a number. Get it free in 60 seconds."
Supporting copy
Connect Instagram → see your Gravity Score, niche rank, and first AI insight. No credit card. Takes 60 seconds. Used by 800+ fitness and lifestyle creators.
2
Screen 1 of 4
Account creation
Target: under 30 seconds
Email + password only. No name, no phone, no credit card. The lighter the signup form, the higher the completion rate. Name and profile details collected later — after the creator has seen value. Google/Apple OAuth available as one-tap alternatives.
Copy principle
The page title is "Your Gravity Score is waiting." Not "Create your account." The framing keeps the promise of value front and center throughout the friction of signup.
3
Screen 2 of 4
Niche selection
Target: under 15 seconds
A single question: "What kind of creator are you?" Six large tap targets. This is not just UX — it's the data that makes benchmarking meaningful. A creator who selects "fitness" immediately gets relevant peer comparisons. No niche = generic benchmarks = less value.
Design spec
Large grid of 6 niche options with icon and label. Single select. Selected state uses Orbit Blue border. One tap — no confirm button needed. Auto-advances to next screen after 600ms delay.
Sub-niche (optional)
After selecting "Fitness", a secondary row appears: strength training, yoga, running, HIIT, nutrition, general. Optional — can skip. The sub-niche improves Constellation matching but isn't required.
4
Screen 3 of 4 — critical moment
Connect Instagram
Target: under 60 seconds
The most important screen in the entire flow. This is where creators either commit or abandon. The frame is not "grant us permissions" — it's "let us calculate your score." Everything on this screen is building toward that specific outcome.
Drop-off risk
The OAuth consent screen is where most onboarding flows lose creators. The Meta OAuth screen looks alarming — it lists all permissions in technical language. Brief the creator before they hit the OAuth screen with a plain-English explanation of exactly what Orbit accesses and why. Reduce surprise = reduce abandonment.
Pre-OAuth screen copy
"To calculate your Gravity Score, Orbit reads:
✓ Your post performance (likes, reach, saves)
✓ Your follower count and growth
✓ Your audience demographics

We never access your messages, passwords, or payment info. You can disconnect at any time."
If they hesitate
Show a small callout: "Used by 800+ fitness creators." Social proof at the moment of hesitation is the most effective conversion tool. Also show a link to the Privacy Policy for those who want detail — don't hide it.
5
Screen 4 of 4 — the wow moment
Gravity Score reveal
Target: under 90 seconds from connect
This is the moment the entire onboarding has been building toward. The Gravity Score animates in — counting up from 0 to the creator's actual score over 1.5 seconds. Below it: niche rank, one benchmark comparison, and a single AI insight. No dashboard clutter. Just the score and what it means.
Animation spec
Score counts up from 0 over 1.5s with an easing curve. Concentric pulse rings radiate from the score as it settles. Niche rank fades in 500ms after score settles. AI insight fades in 800ms after that. Never show a loading spinner on this screen. Pre-calculate in the background while the creator is on the OAuth screen so the reveal is instant.
Score reveal copy
"Your Gravity Score is 72. That puts you in the top 18% of fitness creators at your tier. Here's what's driving it — and what would push it higher."
First AI insight
One specific, data-grounded insight. Never generic. Example: "Your Tuesday posts get 2.1× the engagement of your weekend posts. Your audience is most active on weekday mornings." This is the "I didn't know that" moment.
6
Post-reveal
Benchmarking consent
30 seconds
After the wow moment — and only after — ask for benchmarking opt-in. The creator is now in a positive emotional state having seen value. The ask is honest and clear. This is not pre-checked.
Consent screen copy
Help improve benchmarking for all creators. Your anonymized performance data can be included in our niche benchmarking pool. We never share your individual data — only aggregates. You can opt out any time in Settings.
This is optional. Orbit works fully without it.
7
Post-reveal
Connect second platform (optional)
Optional — skippable
Prompt to connect TikTok if Instagram was primary, or vice versa. Framed as value-add: "See how your content performs differently across platforms." Skip option is prominent — never trap a creator in a multi-step connection flow they didn't ask for.
8
Activation complete
Welcome to the dashboard
Creator is now activated
Land on the dashboard — not a "setup complete" screen. The creator's data is already populated. A single coach tip floats in the bottom right with the week's top recommended action. First Dark Matter briefing email scheduled for the following Monday morning.
Activation definition
A creator is activated when they have: connected at least one platform, seen their Gravity Score, and viewed at least one AI insight. This is the metric to optimize — not account creation. Target: 60% of signups reach activation within 24 hours.

Section 04
Post-signup email sequence

Seven emails over 21 days. Each email has one job: move the creator one step closer to the behaviour that drives retention. After day 21, creators receive only the weekly Dark Matter briefing.

Immediately on signup Your Gravity Score is ready Goal: activation
Triggered the moment the score is calculated. Subject line uses the actual score: "Your Gravity Score is 72 — here's what it means." Body shows the score, niche rank, and links directly to the dashboard. One CTA only: "See your full breakdown →"
CTA: See your full breakdown →
Day 1 The one thing to change this week Goal: first action taken
One specific, actionable recommendation based on their data. Not a feature tour — a real coaching moment. Example: "Your data shows posting Tuesday at 7am would likely increase your reach by 30%. Here's why." Reinforces the "intelligent coach" brand position.
CTA: See your posting analysis →
Day 3 Your Dark Matter signals this week Goal: Dark Matter habit formation
First taste of the Dark Matter briefing — top 3 trending signals in their niche. Teases the weekly briefing as a product they'll want to keep receiving. Shows the value of the Pro tier without mentioning pricing yet.
CTA: See all 10 signals this week →
Day 7 How you compare to the top 10% in your niche Goal: Constellation feature discovery
Show the creator their benchmark position with a teaser — they can see their rank but the detailed gap analysis requires Pro. Framed as: "You're #14 of 840. Here's the one thing that separates the top 10 from everyone else." The gap analysis is behind the paywall.
CTA: See what top creators do differently →
Day 10 Connect TikTok — see the full picture Goal: second platform connection
If only one platform connected, prompt the second. Framed as value: "Your Instagram Gravity Score is 72. Creators who connect TikTok typically see their overall score shift by ±8 points. What's yours?" Not a push — a question that creates curiosity.
CTA: Connect TikTok in 30 seconds →
Day 14 Your Gravity Score this week: 74 (+2) Goal: score improvement reinforcement
A personalised weekly score update. If the score went up — celebrate it and explain why. If it went down — explain what changed and what to do. This email trains creators to check their score regularly and associate Orbit with growth progress.
CTA: See what moved your score →
Day 21 You've been on Orbit 3 weeks — here's your progress Goal: Pro upgrade conversion
A mini performance report showing their progress since joining. What changed. What's still locked on free tier. The upgrade pitch is here — but framed as unlocking more of their own data, not as a sales message. "Your Gravity Score breakdown, full gap analysis, and Escape Velocity alerts are waiting."
CTA: Unlock Pro — $29/month →

Section 06
Talent manager onboarding flow

The manager onboarding is a distinct flow from the creator flow. Managers don't connect their own social accounts — they invite creators to join their roster. The flow must establish trust on both sides: the manager trusts Orbit with sensitive roster data; creators trust that their manager's access is appropriate.

Manager onboarding steps

Step 1
Agency account creation
Same signup as creators but role selection is "Talent manager / agency." Requires agency name and business email domain. Unlocks the Agency pricing tier and Mission Control.
Step 2
Agency profile setup
Agency name, logo upload, primary niche focus (fitness/lifestyle), and roster size estimate. This data is used to configure Mission Control defaults and for benchmarking pool weighting.
Step 3
Invite first creator
Enter creator's email → they receive an invitation to join Orbit under the agency's roster. Creator accepts and completes their own onboarding flow. Manager can see their data once the creator connects a platform and grants access.
Step 4
Mission Control reveal
Once the first creator is onboarded, Mission Control shows the roster overview. Empty state explains what each column shows and what will populate as more creators join. Manager tutorial tooltip sequence walks through the key views.

Creator consent for manager access

Critical trust requirement
Creators must explicitly consent to their manager seeing their data. This cannot be assumed or bypassed. The consent screen must clearly state exactly what the manager can see — and what they cannot.
Creator consent screen copy
Your manager [Name] at [Agency] is inviting you to connect on Orbit.

If you accept, they will be able to see:
✓ Your Gravity Score and history
✓ Your performance metrics and trends
✓ Your benchmark position
✓ Escape velocity alerts

They cannot see:
✗ Your private messages
✗ Your account password
✗ Your billing information

You can revoke access at any time in Settings.

The single metric that predicts everything
If a creator returns to Orbit within 7 days of signup, they have an 80%+ chance of becoming a paying user. If they don't return within 7 days, that probability drops to under 15%. The entire onboarding strategy — the email sequence, the push notifications, the weekly briefing — is designed around driving that day-7 return visit.
