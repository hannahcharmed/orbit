Section 01
Conventions & standards

Naming conventions

ID format
All entity IDs use a prefixed format: prefix_[random 8-char alphanumeric]. Example: crt_abc12345. Prefix list: usr users, crt creators, cnt content, ev escape velocity, sig signals, ins insights, agc agencies, rep reports.
Timestamps
All timestamps stored as TIMESTAMPTZ in UTC. Returned in ISO 8601 format: 2026-03-18T10:00:00Z. Display timezone conversion handled client-side.
Rates & ratios
All rates stored as decimals (0–1). Displayed as percentages in the UI. Example: engagement rate of 6.8% stored as 0.068.

Badge legend

PK Primary key

FK Foreign key reference

IDX Indexed column

NN Not null constraint

Database

PostgreSQL 15 with TimescaleDB extension for time-series tables (content_metrics, gravity_scores, follower_snapshots). All tables use row-level security (RLS) policies enforcing creator data isolation.

Section 13
Metric definitions

Canonical definitions for all computed metrics used across Orbit. These definitions are authoritative — UI labels, API documentation, and AI prompt context must all align to these formulas.

engagement_rate
engagement_rate = (likes + comments + saves + shares) / reach
The primary engagement metric. Normalized against reach (unique accounts) rather than followers, making it platform-agnostic. Saves and shares are weighted equally to likes and comments. Where saves are not available (some platforms), the formula excludes saves.
Source tables: content_metrics. Available platforms: Instagram (full), TikTok (no saves), YouTube (no saves/shares in same form)
gravity_score
gravity_score = round( (return_viewer_rate_score × 0.30) + (save_share_depth_score × 0.25) + (comment_quality_score × 0.20) + (cross_platform_score × 0.15) + (follower_velocity_score × 0.10) )
Composite score 0–100 representing a creator's true audience gravitational pull. Each component is normalized to 0–100 before weighting. Recalculated nightly. Component scores are defined separately below.
return_viewer_rate
return_viewer_rate = unique_returning_viewers_7d / total_unique_viewers_7d
Percentage of viewers who watched content in the past 7 days who have also watched content in the prior 7-day period. Indicates audience loyalty and habit formation. Available from Instagram and YouTube analytics; estimated for TikTok.
save_share_depth
save_share_depth = (saves + (shares × 1.5)) / reach
Weighted rate of high-intent engagement actions. Shares weighted 1.5× saves as they represent both retention (save intent) and amplification. Higher values indicate content worth returning to and spreading.
follower_velocity
follower_velocity = (followers_now - followers_28d_ago) / followers_28d_ago
28-day follower growth rate expressed as a decimal. Measures acceleration of growth, not absolute size. A creator at 100K growing 5% per month has higher velocity than one at 2M growing 0.5%.
baseline_multiple
baseline_multiple = post_engagement_rate / creator_30d_median_engagement_rate
How much a specific post outperforms or underperforms the creator's own 30-day median engagement rate. A baseline_multiple of 2.5 means the post performed 2.5× the creator's typical performance. Used to detect escape velocity events.
momentum_score (Dark Matter)
momentum_score = normalize( usage_growth_7d × 0.50 + niche_relevance × 0.30 + acceleration × 0.20 )
Composite trend signal strength score (0–1). Usage growth is the primary driver (7-day growth rate). Niche relevance is ML-scored based on creator niche matching. Acceleration is the second derivative of usage growth (is it speeding up?). Normalized to 0–1 range.
