Feature 01 — Core differentiator
Gravity Score™
Brand mechanic — gravity = audience pull

Most platforms measure reach. Orbit measures pull — the gravitational force a creator exerts on their audience. Gravity Score is a proprietary composite metric that captures true influence, not just impressions.

What goes into Gravity Score
Return rate — what % of viewers come back within 7 days
Save & share depth — content people return to and pass on
Comment quality — NLP-scored for depth vs shallow reactions
Cross-platform pull — audience following across multiple channels
Follower velocity — rate of acceleration, not just net growth
Why it matters
Brands negotiate on reach. They should negotiate on gravity.
A creator with 200K followers and a Gravity Score of 84 is more valuable than one with 800K and a score of 31. Orbit gives creators and their managers a single, defensible number that reframes every brand deal conversation.
Brand tie-in
The Gravity Score lives on the creator's profile like a vital sign. It becomes the metric Orbit is known for — the number creators reference in pitches, the thing managers optimize for, the standard brands start asking about.

Dark Matter
Brand mechanic — dark matter = hidden opportunity

Dark matter makes up most of the universe but can't be seen directly — it's detected by its gravitational effects. In creator terms, it's the trends building momentum before they become visible. Orbit surfaces them first.

By the time a trend appears on a general trending page, it's already too late. The creators who win are the ones who caught it 2–3 weeks earlier. Dark Matter is Orbit's early warning system.
