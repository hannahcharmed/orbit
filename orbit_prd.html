Section 01
Product overview

Orbit is a creator intelligence platform that unifies cross-platform social analytics, AI-powered coaching, niche benchmarking, and trend intelligence into a single product. It is built specifically for lifestyle and fitness creators and the talent agencies that manage them — a vertical focus that enables depth of insight impossible in general-purpose analytics tools.

Unlike existing tools (Later, Sprout Social, Metricool, Hype Auditor), Orbit is not a scheduling or publishing tool repurposed for analytics. It is analytics and intelligence first — built around the question every creator actually cares about: "What should I do next to grow?"

Problem statement

For creators
Creators track their numbers obsessively but have no framework to interpret them. They don't know if their engagement rate is good or bad, what's driving their best content, or how they compare to true peers in their niche. They're making creative and strategic decisions with incomplete intelligence.
For talent managers
Talent managers juggle 10–50 creators across multiple platforms with no unified tool. They spend hours manually pulling data for brand deal pitches, have no early warning system for creator trajectory changes, and can't easily demonstrate the quantitative value of their roster to brand partners.

Solution summary

Intelligence
AI coaching engine
Personalized recommendations grounded in each creator's actual performance patterns — not generic best practices.
Benchmarking
Constellation View
True niche peer benchmarking against creators with similar following size, platform mix, and content category.
Trends
Dark Matter
Pre-viral trend intelligence filtered to the creator's niche — surfaces emerging audio, formats, and hashtags before they peak.
Measurement
Gravity Score™
A proprietary composite metric that captures true audience pull — return rate, save depth, comment quality, cross-platform following.
Detection
Escape Velocity
Real-time breakout content detection with a 48-hour replication playbook. Capitalizes on the most valuable creative window.
Agency
Mission Control
Multi-creator roster management, alert triage, and auto-generated brand deal performance reports for talent managers.

Users & personas

Orbit serves two distinct user types with different needs, access levels, and use patterns. Both share the same underlying data but interact with it through role-specific interfaces.

Persona 1 — The creator

Primary user
Lifestyle & fitness influencer
Following: 10K–5M across one or more platforms
Platforms: Instagram primary, TikTok secondary, YouTube tertiary
Tech comfort: High — native mobile-first user
Posts per week: 3–14 across platforms
Primary goal: Grow audience, increase brand deal value, understand what content works
Pain point: Knows their numbers but not what they mean or what to do

Jobs to be done

Creator — growth
"As a fitness creator, I want to understand why my last post underperformed so I can avoid making the same mistake next time."
Creator — brand deals
"As a lifestyle influencer, I want a shareable performance report I can send to brands that shows my true engagement value — not just follower count."
Creator — trends
"As a fitness creator, I want to know which audio and formats are gaining momentum in my niche before they become oversaturated."

Persona 2 — The talent manager

Roster size: 5–50 creators under active management
Primary concern: Roster performance, brand deal negotiation, creator development
Tech comfort: Medium — prefers structured dashboards over raw data

Primary goal: Identify opportunities and risks across entire roster quickly

Jobs to be done

"As a talent manager, I want a single daily view of all my creators' performance trajectories so I can prioritize who needs my attention today."
Manager — brand deals
"As an agency operator, I want to auto-generate a polished, data-rich performance deck for any creator on my roster in under 2 minutes."

"As a talent manager, I want to see which of my creators are on a growth trajectory and which are plateauing so I can have proactive strategy conversations."

Section 05
AI & data layer

The AI layer is Orbit's core differentiator. It is not a chatbot or a generic AI assistant — it is a purpose-built intelligence engine trained on creator performance data and calibrated for the lifestyle and fitness niche. Every AI output must be specific, actionable, and grounded in the creator's actual data.

AI coaching engine — requirements

Input signals

Creator's full post history (content type, length, timing, hashtags, audio)
Performance time series (reach, engagement, saves, shares, comments per post)
Audience demographic data from connected platforms
Constellation peer benchmark data (anonymized)
Dark Matter trend signals for creator's niche
Platform algorithm change signals

Output types

Diagnostic insights — "Here's why this post underperformed"
Strategic recommendations — "Based on your data, here's what to do this week"
Escape Velocity playbooks — "Here's how to capitalize on this breakout"
Content briefs — Ready-to-execute post concepts from Dark Matter signals
Trajectory explanations — "Here's what's driving your current path"
Weekly strategy digest — Prioritized action list for the week ahead
AI quality standard
Every AI output must pass a specificity test: could this insight apply to any creator, or is it specific to this creator's data? Generic outputs ("Post more consistently") are a product failure. Every recommendation must reference specific data points from the creator's own performance history.

API risk management
Platform APIs are subject to change without notice. All integrations must be built with an abstraction layer so platform-specific changes can be patched without affecting the core data model. Rate limiting, quota management, and graceful degradation are required for all integrations.

Section 08
UX & design requirements

Design principles

Intelligence over data
Every screen should answer "so what?" not just "what." Raw numbers must always be accompanied by context — benchmark comparison, trend direction, or AI interpretation.
Action over observation
Every insight must lead to an action. The product fails if a creator learns something from Orbit and doesn't know what to do with it.
Coach, not dashboard
The tone, language, and UX framing should feel like a brilliant strategist giving advice — not a spreadsheet delivering numbers.

Section 09
Security & privacy

Creator data is sensitive and personal. Privacy and security are non-negotiable requirements, not afterthoughts. Orbit's data practices must be transparent, defensible, and creator-controlled.

Data handling requirements

Data minimization — only collect data explicitly needed for product functionality
Explicit consent — creators must actively opt in to benchmarking data pool; opt-in cannot be default
Data portability — creators can export all their data in CSV/JSON at any time
Right to deletion — complete data deletion within 30 days of account closure
No third-party data sale — Orbit will never sell individual creator data to third parties
Aggregate only — benchmarking pool data used only in aggregate; no individual peer data exposed

Security requirements

Encryption at rest — AES-256 encryption for all stored data
Encryption in transit — TLS 1.3 for all API communications
OAuth token security — platform tokens stored encrypted, never logged, rotated on schedule
Role-based access — strict separation between creator and manager data access
SOC 2 Type II — target compliance within 18 months of launch
GDPR compliance — required for EU creator base
Penetration testing — annual third-party security audit

Section 12
Risks & mitigations
RiskSeverityLikelihoodMitigation
Platform API restrictions — TikTok or Instagram restricts data access or changes API terms High Medium Abstraction layer isolates platform changes. Maintain relationships with platform developer programs. TikTok partnership application from day 1. Always have 2+ platforms live so no single API failure breaks the product.
Benchmarking cold start — insufficient peer data to make Constellation benchmarks meaningful at launch Medium High Agency roster (50+ creators) seeds the benchmark pool. Supplement with third-party data provider (Modash, HypeAuditor API) for early-stage gaps. Set clear minimum peer count thresholds before showing benchmarks.
AI insight quality — AI recommendations are generic or inaccurate, eroding creator trust High Medium Specificity testing protocol for all AI outputs. Human review of first 1,000 AI insights. Thumbs up/down feedback loop built into UI from day 1. Continuous model refinement based on feedback data.
Creator churn — creators don't see tangible growth results and cancel High Medium Onboarding flow sets clear expectations. Weekly digest keeps creators engaged even in slow weeks. Escape Velocity alerts create "wow moments" that drive retention. Track time-to-first-value carefully.
Data privacy incident — creator data breach or unauthorized access Critical Low SOC 2 compliance roadmap from day 1. Third-party pen testing before public launch. Incident response plan documented and rehearsed. Cyber liability insurance. Transparent privacy policy with clear data practices.
Competitor response — Later, Sprout Social, or a well-funded startup copies core features Medium Medium Data moat from agency-seeded benchmarking is the primary defense — it takes years to replicate. Speed of iteration matters. Gravity Score™ trademark filing. Brand and community are defensible assets beyond features.
