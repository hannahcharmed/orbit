The arc principle

The mark is a fading orbital arc — a single path that gains intensity at the leading edge (the data point) and fades as it completes the circle. At small sizes the fade disappears, leaving a clean bold arc and dot. Never crop, rotate, or modify the arc.

Logo clearspace & misuse

Correct usage
✓ Minimum clearspace = height of the letter O on all sides
✓ Always use approved color combinations
✓ Maintain minimum width of 80px for full lockup
✓ Mark only permitted below 80px width
✓ Wordmark always set in Georgia serif, letter-spacing 5px
Never do this
✗ Don't stretch, skew, or rotate the mark
✗ Don't change the wordmark typeface
✗ Don't place on a busy photographic background
✗ Don't use unapproved colors on the mark
✗ Don't add effects (shadows, outlines, glows)

Section 06
Voice & tone

Brand voice

Orbit sounds like a brilliant strategist who happens to have all the data. Direct, specific, never generic. Every sentence should answer "so what?" — not just describe what happened.

Marketing
"Every creator has a gravitational pull. We measure it."
Orbit AI insight
"Your Tuesday Reel hit escape velocity — performing 6.2× above your baseline. Post a follow-up within 48 hours using the same format. Creators who capitalize on escape velocity events within 48hrs see 2.3× higher sustained growth."
Onboarding
"Your Gravity Score is 78. That puts you in the top 12% of fitness creators at your tier. Here's what's pulling it up — and what would push it higher."
Empty state
"Connect Instagram to see your Gravity Score. Takes 30 seconds."
Sound like this
Specific and grounded in data
Direct — subject, verb, impact
Confident without being arrogant
Uses "your" — possessive, personal
Tells you what to do next
Short sentences. White space. Impact.
Never sound like this
"Post more consistently to grow"
"Our AI-powered platform leverages..."
"We're excited to announce..."
"Data-driven insights for creators"
Passive voice or corporate hedging
Emoji in professional contexts

Section 08
Motion principles

Motion in Orbit should feel like the precision of orbital mechanics — purposeful, measured, and never decorative. Every animation should reinforce the product's intelligence.

Ease default
cubic-bezier(0.4, 0, 0.2, 1)
Material standard ease. Used for all state transitions — hover, focus, open/close. Feels responsive without being snappy.
Duration scale
100 / 200 / 300 / 500ms
100ms: micro (hover). 200ms: UI transitions. 300ms: panels and modals. 500ms: page-level and chart animations.
Orbit Path animation
stroke-dashoffset · 1.2s linear
The orbital arc animates by drawing the stroke from the data point forward. Never spins in full circles — always a partial arc in motion.
Chart entrance
fade + translateY(8px) · 300ms
Data elements enter from slightly below with a fade. Bars grow upward from baseline. Lines draw left to right at 500ms.
Gravity pulse
opacity 0.3→0.7 · 2.5s ease
Concentric ring pulse used for the Gravity Score indicator and live data states. Slow, meditative — not urgent.
Reduced motion
@media(prefers-reduced-motion)
All animations wrapped in this media query. Opacity transitions remain; transforms and draws are disabled for accessibility.
